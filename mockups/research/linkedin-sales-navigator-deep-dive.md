# LinkedIn Sales Navigator — deep research brief

Compiled June 10, 2026 from linkedin.com help/business pages (indexed), Microsoft filings, and third-party 2026 analyses.

## 1. Pricing (2026; restructured late 2025)

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Adds |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core | $119.99 | ~$90/seat/mo | Advanced search, saved searches + alerts, lists, 50 InMails/mo, Notes |
| Advanced | $160–180 | ~$140/seat/mo | Smart Links, TeamLink/Extend, CSV account upload, buyer intent, Relationship Explorer, admin/SSO |
| Advanced Plus | Custom | ~$1,300–1,600+/seat/yr, ~10-seat min | CRM Sync (Salesforce, Dynamics, HubSpot), data validation, activity write-back, ROI reporting |

30-day trial on Core/Advanced (card required, auto-renews); no Advanced Plus trial. Volume discounts ~10–20% at 5+ seats.

## 2. Mechanics

- **Search**: ~40+ lead filters (incl. "changed jobs in 90 days," "posted in 30 days," "following your company," TeamLink paths) + account filters (headcount growth, revenue, technologies, hiring, buyer intent). Saved searches re-run continuously with alerts (job changes, posts, funding, intent spikes).
- **Caps**: **2,500 visible leads per search** (100 pages × 25), 1,000 accounts; **no native CSV export of leads, ever**. CSV *upload* of account lists (Advanced+): ~1,000 rows/20MB, auto-matched.
- **InMail**: 50/mo all tiers, rollover cap 150, credit refunded if reply within 90 days. The only native outreach channel.
- **Smart Links** (Advanced+): trackable content with viewer-level analytics; doubles as soft intent.
- **TeamLink / Extend**: colleagues' 1st-degree networks as warm paths; Extend opts in non-seat employees.
- **Relationship Explorer**: ~8 most relevant leads per account vs your Persona.
- **Buyer Intent**: 180+ first-party signals (profile views, follows, ad interactions, InMail accepts across your contract) → account intent scores; plus Product Category Intent.
- **AI**: Account IQ / Lead IQ (genAI summaries), Message Assist (AI InMails), AI-assisted search, **Sales Assistant** (agentic, beta 2024 → rollout 2025–26).

## 3. CRM integration (Advanced Plus only for sync)

- CRMs: Salesforce, Dynamics 365, **HubSpot** (added ~2024).
- CRM → SN: auto-creates "My CRM accounts" lists, flags in-CRM leads, at-risk-opportunity alerts.
- SN → CRM: auto-logs InMails/messages/Smart Link activity (basis of ROI reporting).
- **Data validation**: flags CRM contacts who left the company (writes a "no longer at company" CRM field) — unique hygiene/champion-tracking signal.
- Embedded profiles in SFDC/Dynamics on Advanced+.

## 4. What it does NOT do

- No verified emails, no phones, no dialer, no email infrastructure, no sequencing/cadences, no lead export.
- **ToS hard line**: bots, scrapers, and extensions that "scrape, modify the appearance of, or automate activity" are prohibited; paying for Sales Nav confers no immunity. Q1 2026: detection update — suspicious sessions flagged within ~48h, **suspension-first** enforcement; ban waves hit automation-tool users.

## 5. Reviews

G2 ~4.4/5 (~2,100+). Pros: unmatched reach, real-activity intent/alerts, filtering. Cons: price (raised repeatedly), clunky messaging UX, data locked in (no emails/export), CRM sync paywalled at top tier.

## 6. Business facts

- LinkedIn (Microsoft since 2016, $26.2B). LinkedIn revenue $5.08B in FQ2 FY26 (+11%) → ~$19–20B run rate; 1.2B members. Sales Nav not broken out; est. **1M+ subscribers, very roughly $1.5–2B+ ARR**.
- 2024–26: Account IQ/Lead IQ, Message Assist, Relationship Explorer, Product Category Intent, HubSpot sync, Sales Assistant; late-2025 price restructure.

## 7. Strategic position

- **The walled garden is the business model** — no emails/export/API isn't a gap, it's the moat. The adjacent market (Apollo, Lusha, Clay, Lemlist, extensions) monetizes the deliberate gap, at LinkedIn's sufferance.
- **SNAP** (the only official API; Display/Sync/Analytics, partner-only, no data extraction) — **new applications frozen since Aug 20, 2025**, no reopen date. No sanctioned programmatic path for startups.
- A copilot that helps a human use Sales Nav better is economically a **complement** (raises seat value); the risk is policy, not economics — extensions that read/modify the Sales Nav DOM sit inside the prohibited-software language. Meanwhile LinkedIn builds its own copilot (Sales Assistant) — squeezing assistive UX from above while bans squeeze automation from below.
- The two structurally unreplicable assets: the **self-updating employment graph** and **first-party behavioral intent**. Sales Nav stays the substrate; everything else stays a layer.

## Strategic flags for KomboAI

1. Build strictly assistive — no scraping, no auto-actions; users' LinkedIn accounts are the asset to protect under suspension-first enforcement.
2. We sell exactly what the garden withholds: contact data, sequencing, live coaching, CRM hygiene.
3. Watch Sales Assistant's rollout — LinkedIn moving up the assist stack compresses the copilot niche over time; our moat must stay in the call + CRM, where LinkedIn won't go.
