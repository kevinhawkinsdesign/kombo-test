Unique selling proposition

The fastest one-click reveal of verified emails and direct dials, powered by a crowd-sourced data community of 800K+ sales pros — a give-to-get network effect that compounds coverage and accuracy (80–90% email accuracy in 2026 testing).

Ideal customer profile

SDRs, AEs and recruiters at SMB-to-mid-market teams — especially US tech & SaaS. Bottom-up buyers who start free on the extension and expand into seats.

Signature features
  • Chrome extension reveal on LinkedIn, Sales Nav & the open web
  • Buying-signal & funding alerts to time outreach
  • Bulk reveal + AI recommendations
  • Bolt-on sequencing (Engage) & call recording (Conversations)
Pricing model

Freemium + credits. Free tier (~70 credits/mo); Pro from ~$29/mo. Each reveal burns credits (1 email / 10 phone), which nudges expansion as usage grows.

Growth & marketing engine

Product-led, bottom-up virality. The free extension is the wedge; the crowd-sourced database is the moat. Lusha dogfoods its own product for GTM and recently grew ICP signups 16% while cutting paid spend 50% — leaning into efficient, ICP-focused acquisition over spray-and-pray paid media. Backed by a $205M Series B (PSG) at a $1.5B valuation.

For KomboAI A free, viral extension tier is a proven wedge — and a crowd-sourced data flywheel is genuinely defensible. The gap: Lusha stops at data. We act on it.

Head-to-head deep dives

Where each competitor genuinely shines — and the specific job KomboAI is built to do differently.

KomboAI vs Lusha
Copilot vs contact database
Where Lusha wins

Raw contact coverage. With 280M+ verified profiles and a slick Chrome extension, Lusha is one of the fastest ways to reveal a direct dial or email mid-prospecting. Independent 2026 testing puts email accuracy at 80–90%. If your only job is "get me the number," it's hard to beat.

Where KomboAI differs

Lusha hands you data; KomboAI acts on it. The same enrichment is wired into multichannel sequences, live call coaching, and automatic CRM entry — so a revealed contact becomes a booked meeting without a second tool. Lusha's sequencing and coaching are bolt-ons, not the core loop.

KomboAI vs Lemlist
Full loop vs send engine
Where Lemlist wins

Pure outbound craft. Lemlist's Lemwarm network, granular A/B testing, and image/video personalization make it a deliverability-obsessed sender's favourite, now spanning email, LinkedIn, calls, WhatsApp and SMS from one campaign at a friendly per-seat price.

Where KomboAI differs

Lemlist stops at the reply. KomboAI carries the deal through the call and the CRM — coaching reps live, capturing notes, and updating records automatically. You're comparing a best-in-class send engine to an end-to-end rep copilot.

KomboAI vs Amplemarket
Accessible vs premium suite
Where Amplemarket wins

A polished, opinionated all-in-one. Its Duo AI agents (Signal, Research, Sequence) and voice cloning produce notably human outreach, and it carries a strong 4.6/5 G2 rating with standout support. For a funded team that wants one vendor, it's compelling.

Where KomboAI differs

Amplemarket starts at ~$600/mo, annual-only — a real commitment before you've seen results. KomboAI bills per rep with a monthly option and a trial, adds live conversation coaching Amplemarket doesn't, and meets reps inside the tools they already live in rather than a new workspace.

KomboAI vs Clay
Rep-ready vs ops toolkit
Where Clay wins

Unmatched enrichment depth and flexibility. Waterfall enrichment across 150+ providers plus the Claygent research agent and programmable, spreadsheet-style workflows make Clay the power tool for technical RevOps teams building bespoke data pipelines.

Where KomboAI differs

Clay is a builder's canvas — powerful, but it expects someone to architect and maintain it, and costs run ~$0.65–1.20 per contact at scale. KomboAI is rep-ready out of the box: the enrichment, outreach and coaching are pre-wired, so individual sellers get value on day one without an ops engineer.

Most of the field optimises one step of outbound. KomboAI is built to compress the whole loop — find, reach, talk, log — into one place reps never have to leave.

Comparison FAQ

Is this comparison biased toward KomboAI?
It's our page, so naturally we explain where KomboAI fits best — but we've kept every competitor's strengths accurate and front-and-centre, with public 2026 pricing and product positioning. Lusha, Lemlist, Amplemarket and Clay are each genuinely excellent at their core job; the matrix reflects standard product scope, not edge-case add-ons.
Why does KomboAI show "partial" for a built-in contact database?
KomboAI focuses on enriching and acting on contacts through multi-source waterfall enrichment rather than selling raw database access the way Lusha or Amplemarket do. If a standalone, high-volume contact database is your primary need, a data-first tool may suit better — many teams use both.
Can KomboAI replace several of these tools at once?
For most sales teams, yes — the goal is to fold enrichment, multichannel outreach, conversation coaching and CRM auto-fill into one loop so you're not paying for and switching between four products. Highly technical RevOps teams with bespoke pipelines may still keep a tool like Clay alongside.
How current is the pricing shown?
Figures reflect publicly listed pricing as reviewed in June 2026. Vendors change pricing frequently — Clay overhauled its credit model in March 2026, for example — so always confirm on the vendor's own pricing page before purchasing.
